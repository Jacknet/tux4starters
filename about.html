About


Tux For Starters is the result of a Senior Project course at the University of South Florida (USF) during Spring of 2021 where the group, The EDU Liberators, created this website to provide students who are new to the world of UNIX and Linux operating systems a means to be able to learn about them.

Who created this?

The EDU Liberators are made up of the following USF students: Joaquin Padilla, Mayank Joshi, Justin Asbury, and Alexis Herrera.

How does it work?

Students are tasked with working on lessons that have them reading about these types of systems while also being able to interact with a fascimile of a terminal to get them adjusted to using one if they decide to try out any of the operating systems discussed.
Students are ranked on whether they were able to complete the assignment or answer any multiple choice questions without errors. As they progress from lesson to lesson, they will have a means of tracking which items are completed through markers and color coding on the main lessons page and after they have completed a lesson.

How do I get started as a student?

You can get started by creating an account and then heading to the Lessons area to begin your journey!

How do I find out more about UNIX/Linux?

Head over to our Resources section to find more information on these topics and further training.

How do I contribute to this project?

Contributions to this project can be made via our Github repository where you will need to be invited to make contributions. All contributions are vetted in order to prevent any errors or issues that may occur.
