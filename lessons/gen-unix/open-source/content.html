General UNIX | What is Open Source?

With the amount of distributions, you're probably wondering, "Is that legal?", and the answer is "Yes, but there's a catch".


Distributions will usually be created by a group of people and will follow some or most of what was written previously by users. Some of these distributions can have their own copyrights for certain items, but for the most part, most of these distributions are free for anyone to download, install, and play around with.


Software that allows users to mess around with it, create different versions of, work with people online, and not charge one cent for, are called Open Source.


Open Source is a different approach to software distribution. Instead of receiving just the final product, you are also supplied the source code that was used to construct it.
