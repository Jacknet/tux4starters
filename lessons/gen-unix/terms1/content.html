General UNIX | The Terminal

The Terminal Emulator lets users interact with the shell. It's called a terminal emulator because it emulates the functions of the terminal to do certain things, you are essentially acting as if you are the computer telling it what to do. We can do a lot of neat things with the terminal emulator that comes packaged with many distributions, such as create files, move files, write, and make changes to different folders and items.


A great way to look at this would be like you are controlling a whole factory where robots are putting pieces together to make cars. You can tell each robot to fasten regular doors or gullwing doors (doors that go up), you can even tell it to paint the car a certain color. Wait, you didn't want that color, you wanted another color - so you can undo those changes.


In a terminal, these changes can be made just by typing commands instead of dragging and dropping items to different areas.
